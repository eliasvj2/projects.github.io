Iceland

Iceland is a country of sharp contrasts. A place where fire and ice co-exist. Where dark winters are offset by the summer's midnight sun. A country where insular existence had spurred a rich and vibrant culture.

Things to do in Iceland

When making a trip to Iceland, it is hard not to pay special attention to the country's namesake--namely, its 4,500 square miles of glacier. Ice climbing on the glaciers is practiced year-round and takes place mainly on the Solheimajokull and Svinafellsjokull glaciers in the south of Iceland, to which day trips are offered from Reykjavik (and Skaftfell).

The Aurora Borealis takes its name from the Roman goddes of dawn, Aurora, and the Greek name for the north wind, Boreaux. From september to april, Iceland is a hotspot for this magnificent lighthow.

The Aurora Borealis, more commonly referred to as the Northen Lights is a natural phenomenon created when particles emitted by the sun interact with the atmosphere in the Earth's magnetic filed. This releases energy, causing peculiar luminous green streaks across the skies.
